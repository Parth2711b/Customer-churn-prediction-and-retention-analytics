Project Objective

Identify customers most likely to churn, explain the drivers of churn, and convert insights into an operational retention prioritization framework.

Business Problem

  • Churn reduces revenue, balances, and long-term customer lifetime value.
  • Retention resources are limited and must be prioritized.
  • The business needs explainable risk drivers, not only a black-box score.
  • Managers need a dashboard for monitoring churn patterns and risk segments.

Methodology

  • Use actual churn outcome Exited to calculate churn rates by customer segment.
  • Rank churn drivers by observed churn lift and business relevance.
  • Translate top drivers into a transparent 0–100 score.
  • Create tiers: Low, Medium, High, Critical.
  • Build dashboard visuals for decision support and retention targeting.
The model is explainable by design: every risk point maps to a known churn driver.

RiskTier converts individual customer scores into retention campaign priority groups.

Model Outcome and Use

  • Output file adds RiskScore and RiskTier to every customer record.
  • RiskScore enables customer-level prioritization.
  • RiskTier enables portfolio-level monitoring and campaign planning.
  • Score components explain why a customer is high-risk.
Best operational use: build daily or weekly High/Critical customer queues for retention teams.

Dashboard Design

  • KPI cards: total customers, churned customers, churn rate, retained customers.
  • Driver charts: product count, age band, geography, activity, balance, gender.
  • Interaction chart: inactive seniors and female-Germany risk groups.
  • Risk-score design chart: explains point budget to business users.
  • Action panel: recommends retention plays by segment.

Business Recommendations

  • Prioritize High/Critical customers for immediate outreach.
  • Investigate 3–4 product customers for product overload, misfit, or service friction.
  • Launch 50–59 care journey with relationship-manager contact and loyalty support.
  • Create Germany-specific retention actions based on local churn patterns.
  • Reactivate inactive customers with personalized engagement and financial health checkups.
